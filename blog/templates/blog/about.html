    Who: Spencer Tollefson

    Who is Spencer Tollefson? He's a human being aspiring to land a data scientist job. Currently working for the Peace Corps, the awesome gig has had him living in rural Costa Rica since February 2017. Pura vida! Like many other Seattle-ites Spencer is big into craft beer, doesn't mind the rain too much, and oh yeah - he's into tech. Python is his language and manipulating data is his game. </bad_joke> But hang on, honesty first policy here: Spencer only began learning to code in October 2017 (He's good at Excel though, that counts for something right?!) To help him learn any and everything about data science, he has been chowing down on blogs, podcasts, tutorials, videos, and more as if he does to sweet Costa Rican papayas.

    Non-tech stuff? Dabbles in body weight fitness exercises and loves long walks - not kidding! Watches, analyzes, and debates NBA (the Sonics will rise again). Subscribes to the Crash Course YouTube channel. Reads non-fiction and Wikipedia for pleasure. Eats mostly "unprocessed" plants. Finally, Spencer organizes his life by maintaining lists on Google Keep of everything he can quantify.

    The Site

    So what is this website and why did Spencer make it? Not going to beat around the bush here - his primary reason for building this site is for himself. He is using it for motivation, to track what he's learned, to keep a tech journal, and to expand on topics that capture his attention. Of course that's not the only reason though. The site in itself is a project - Spencer's first (and perhaps last) web dev project - and he's learned a ton every step of creating this baby. Additionally, Spencer wants to connect with like-minded people and maintain a portfolio for potential opportunities.

    He built this site in Python using the Django framework for architecture and the Bootstrap Content Delivery Network for support in the layout. It's deployed via Heroku. It was quite the challenge, and Spencer doubts he'll ever make money being a web dev, but hey, besides what he learned he has something to show for it as well!
