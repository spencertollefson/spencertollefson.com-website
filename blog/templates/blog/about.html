    Me

    Hi - I'm a human being aspiring to land a data scientist job. Right now I work/volunteer for the Peace Corps and the awesome gig has had me living in rural Costa Rica since February 2017. Pura vida! Like many other Seattle-ites I'm big into craft beer, don't mind the rain too much, and oh yeah - I'm into tech. Python is my language and manipulating data is my game. But hang on, I'll be honest - I only began learning to code in October 2017 (I'm good at Excel though, that counts for something right?!) To help me learn any and everything pertaining to data science and Python here in Costa Rica, I've been chowing down on blogs, podcasts, tutorials, videos, and more at a pace only sustainable in my current Costa Rican lifestyle.

    Non-tech stuff? Dabble in body weight and love long walks (not kidding!) NBA fan (RIP Sonics). Subscribe to the Crash Course YouTube channel. Read non-fiction and Wikipedia for pleasure. Eat mostly "unprocessed" plants. Finally, I organize my life by making lists on Google Keep lists of nearly everything.

    The Site

    So what is this website and why did I make it? Not going to beat around the bush here - my primary reason for building this site is for myself. I'm using it as a way to keep motivated, track what I've learned, keep a tech journal, and expand on topics that capture my attention via blog posts. Of course that's not the only reason though. The site in itself is a project - my first web dev project (and perhaps my last?) - and I've learned a ton every step of creating this baby. Additionally, I want to connect with like-minded people and maintain a portfolio for potential opportunities.

    I built this site in Python using the Django framework for architecture and the Bootstrap Content Delivery Network for support in the layout. It's deployed via Heroku. This was quite the challenge, and I doubt I'll ever make money being a web dev, but hey, besides what I learned I have something to show for it as well!
